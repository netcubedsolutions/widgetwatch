Frequently Asked Questions

How many aircraft does Delta Air Lines have?
Delta Air Lines operates a mainline fleet of 1004 aircraft as of March 2026, spanning 17 aircraft types including the Airbus A220, A319, A320, A321, A330, and A350 families, plus the Boeing 717, 737, 757, and 767. This does not include Delta Connection regional aircraft operated by partner carriers.
Which Delta Air Lines planes have Delta One business class?
Delta One lie-flat business class is available on all widebody international aircraft: Airbus A350-900 (40), A330-900neo (39), A330-300 (31), A330-200 (11), Boeing 767-400ER (21), and 767-300ER (37). That's 179 aircraft with Delta One seats. The 767-400ER and all three A330/A350 variants also feature Delta Premium Select Premium economy. Use the interactive fleet database to check the exact seat configuration for any specific aircraft by registration number.
What is the largest aircraft in Delta's fleet?
The Airbus A350-900 is the largest aircraft in Delta Air Lines' fleet, seating up to 306 passengers across four cabin classes: Delta One, Delta Premium Select, Delta Comfort+, and Main Cabin. Delta operates 40 A350-900s, primarily on flagship long-haul international routes from hubs like Atlanta (ATL) and New York JFK (JFK).
